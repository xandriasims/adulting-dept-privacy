Privacy Policy

BASCO Content Ops · Operated by The Adulting Dept · Effective July 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how BASCO Content Ops accesses, uses, stores, and deletes information when a creator connects a social-media account or uses the content operations dashboard.

Information you provide

The application stores content and operational information that you choose to provide, such as brand details, content ideas, drafts, captions, source notes, publishing selections, and schedules.

Information from connected platforms

The application accesses only the information needed to connect authorized accounts, import their own public content, and report aggregate performance:

  • TikTok: the authorized user's open ID and display name; their public videos and video metadata; and counts such as views, likes, comments, and shares. The application does not access private messages, followers' identities, or non-public videos.
  • Meta and Threads: authorized Page or account identifiers, names or handles, the account's own published posts, public post information, and available aggregate insights such as views, likes, comments, saves, replies, reposts, and shares.
  • YouTube: public channel and video information available through the YouTube Data API, including titles, descriptions, publication dates, views, likes, and comments. The current integration uses an API key and does not request access to a user's private Google account data.

How information is used

Information is used to operate features you request: organizing content, drafting, publishing or scheduling, importing account history, displaying performance, comparing results over time, and maintaining authorized account connections. Connected-platform data is not sold, used for advertising, or used to build profiles about followers or other people.

Storage and security

Content, public post history, and aggregate metrics are stored in a local database on the application operator's computer. Access tokens, refresh tokens, client secrets, and API keys are stored in the operating system's protected keychain on that computer rather than in the public website or source code. Reasonable technical measures are used to limit access, but no storage method can be guaranteed completely secure.

Service providers and disclosure

The application sends information to a connected platform when necessary to perform an action you request. If enabled by the operator, Blotato processes content for publishing and Anthropic processes supplied draft context to generate text. TikTok account data and video metrics are not sent to Anthropic for model training. Information may also be disclosed when required by law or necessary to protect rights, safety, and security. Personal information is not sold or rented.

Retention

Locally stored content and metrics remain until the operator deletes the applicable records or local database. Platform credentials remain in the protected keychain until they expire, are replaced, or are deleted. Revoking access at a platform prevents future collection but does not automatically erase information already stored locally.

Your choices and data deletion

You may revoke BASCO Content Ops in the connected platform's app-permission settings at any time. To remove information held by the application, delete the applicable local records or database and remove the related credentials from the operating system keychain. You may also request assistance by emailing toya.a.dorsey@gmail.com. Requests will be handled after reasonable verification that the requester controls the relevant account.

Children's privacy

BASCO Content Ops is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

This public policy site

This policy site does not contain account login, forms, advertising, or application analytics. It is hosted through GitHub Pages, so GitHub may process standard technical request information under its own privacy terms when the page is visited.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the application or connected services change. Material revisions will be published here with an updated effective date.

Contact
