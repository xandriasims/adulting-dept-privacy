Privacy Policy

The Adulting Dept — content operations tool · Effective July 16, 2026

This application is a personal content-management tool operated by The Adulting Dept. It exists to draft, publish, and measure The Adulting Dept's own social media posts. It has a single user: the account owner.

What data the app accesses

Through the Meta (Facebook and Instagram) APIs, the app accesses only data belonging to The Adulting Dept's own accounts: the list of Pages the owner manages, the linked Instagram professional account, the account's own published posts, and aggregate performance insights for those posts (such as likes, comments, saves, shares, and views).

What the app does not do

  • It does not collect, store, or process data about any person other than the account owner.
  • It does not access followers' identities, messages, or personal information.
  • It does not sell, share, license, or transfer any data to third parties.
  • It does not use data for advertising, profiling, or training machine-learning models.

Where data is stored

Post content and aggregate metrics are stored in a local database on the account owner's own computer. Access tokens are stored in the operating system's keychain on that same computer. Nothing is stored on servers operated by this application.

Data deletion

All data the app holds can be deleted at any time by the account owner by deleting the local database and revoking the app's access in Facebook Settings → Apps and Websites. Because the app stores no data about anyone other than its owner, there is no third-party data to request deletion of.
